\begin{frame}{The Conditional Theorem}
\protect\hypertarget{the-conditional-theorem}{}

These two things are equivalent in Carnap for any \(A, B, C\).

\begin{enumerate}
\tightlist
\item
  \(A, B \vdash C\)
\item
  \(A \vdash B \rightarrow C\)
\end{enumerate}

This is somewhat intuitive, though it's worth going over why it is so.

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Argument for 1 to 2 (Informal)}
\protect\hypertarget{argument-for-1-to-2-informal}{}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Assume we have a proof from \(A, B\) to \(C\)
\item
  Now imagine a proof that starts with \(A\), then has `Show:
  \(B \rightarrow C\)' followed by an assumption of \(B\).
\item
  Whatever you did in the first proof to get to \(C\), you can repeat in
  the second proof.
\item
  So eventually, you'll get \(C\).
\item
  So you can now use `Conditional Derivation' to get
  \(B \rightarrow C\).
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Argument for 2 to 1}
\protect\hypertarget{argument-for-2-to-1}{}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Assume that from \(A\) we have a proof of \(B \rightarrow C\)
\item
  Continue that proof for one more line with premise \(B\).
\item
  Then use MPP on the last two lines to get \(C\).
\item
  And now you've got a proof with premises \(A, B\) and conclusion
  \(C\).
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Metalogic}
\protect\hypertarget{metalogic}{}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  The last two slides were super-duper informal.
\item
  But the basic arguments were sound.
\item
  And this is how you prove results in \textbf{metalogic}.
\item
  You aren't trying to prove that some particular things are true,
  you're trying to prove general things about the nature of what can be
  proven.
\item
  We're not going to do much metalogic in this course, but it's worth
  knowing about.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}
